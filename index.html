Microbreweries typically produce fewer than 15,000 barrels (460,000 gallons) of beer annually. More often however, the term 'microbrewery' is used to reflect an alternative attitude and approach to brewing flexibility, adaptability, experimentation and customer service.

Brewpubs are pubs or restaurants that brew beer on the premises. In the United States a brewpub is defined as selling 25 percent or more of its beer on-site and operating significant food services.

Regional breweries are the regional locations of an expanded, multi-location brewery.

Nanobreweries are loosely defined as a scaled-down microbrewery, often producing beer in very small, limited batches.
